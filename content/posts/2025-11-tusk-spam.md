I've been getting spam emails about selling or monitizing Tusk almost since it was published in 2018. Usually it's one of two offers:

* People wanting to buy the extension outright
* People wanting to pay you to install their "SDK" that does some kind of ad injection or search hijacking or data mining or all of the above.

Some come from sketchy gmail accounts, some from semi-professional looking domains. One in particular, Ads-Connect.co.uk, was actually incorporated in the UK from 2020 until it was dissolved in 2022 and had employees on LinkedIn.

The first account I can find is from [Amit Agarwal in Jan 2014](https://www.labnol.org/internet/sold-chrome-extension/28377).

> It was a 4-figure offer for something that had taken an hour to create and I agreed to the deal. I had no clue about the buyer and was also curious to know why would anyone pay this kind of money for such a simple Chrome extension.

My offers, taken at face value, would similarly shell out thousands for Tusk at 10 - 20 cents per user. Depending on the developer's financial situation, that could be real cash.

Anyway, here's a sample of the messages I've gotten over the years.
